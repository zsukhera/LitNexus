“It is not my fault that I cannot eat or rest,” he replied.
“I assure you it is through no settled designs. I’ll do both, as
soon as I possibly can. But you might as well bid a man struggling in the water
rest within arms’ length of the shore! I must reach it first, and then
I’ll rest. Well, never mind Mr. Green: as to repenting of my injustices,
I’ve done no injustice, and I repent of nothing. I’m too happy; and
yet I’m not happy enough. My soul’s bliss kills my body, but does
not satisfy itself.”
