Mr. Heathcliff, who grew more and more disinclined to society, had almost
banished Earnshaw from his apartment. Owing to an accident at the commencement
of March, he became for some days a fixture in the kitchen. His gun burst while
out on the hills by himself; a splinter cut his arm, and he lost a good deal of
blood before he could reach home. The consequence was that, perforce, he was
condemned to the fireside and tranquillity, till he made it up again. It suited
Catherine to have him there: at any rate, it made her hate her room upstairs
more than ever: and she would compel me to find out business below, that she
might accompany me.
