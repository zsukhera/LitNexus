“Never mind!” I answered, tartly. And I told him how she had
resolved, previous to his coming, on exhibiting a fit of frenzy. I incautiously
gave the account aloud, and she heard me; for she started up—her hair
flying over her shoulders, her eyes flashing, the muscles of her neck and arms
standing out preternaturally. I made up my mind for broken bones, at least; but
she only glared about her for an instant, and then rushed from the room. The
master directed me to follow; I did, to her chamber-door: she hindered me from
going further by securing it against me.
