“You are aware, Mr. Heathcliff,” I said, “that from the time
you were thirteen years old you have lived a selfish, unchristian life; and
probably hardly had a Bible in your hands during all that period. You must have
forgotten the contents of the book, and you may not have space to search it
now. Could it be hurtful to send for some one—some minister of any
denomination, it does not matter which—to explain it, and show you how
very far you have erred from its precepts; and how unfit you will be for its
heaven, unless a change takes place before you die?”
