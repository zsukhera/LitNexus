“But Mr. Heathcliff was quite cordial, papa,” observed Catherine,
not at all convinced; “and he didn’t object to our seeing
each other: he said I might come to his house when I pleased; only I must not
tell you, because you had quarrelled with him, and would not forgive him for
marrying aunt Isabella. And you won’t. You are the one to be
blamed: he is willing to let us be friends, at least; Linton and I; and
you are not.”
