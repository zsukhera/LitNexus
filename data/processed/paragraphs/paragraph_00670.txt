“Yes; she’s in the kitchen, sir,” I answered.
“She’s sadly put out by Mr. Heathcliff’s behaviour: and,
indeed, I do think it’s time to arrange his visits on another footing.
There’s harm in being too soft, and now it’s come to
this—.” And I related the scene in the court, and, as near as I
dared, the whole subsequent dispute. I fancied it could not be very prejudicial
to Mrs. Linton; unless she made it so afterwards, by assuming the defensive for
her guest. Edgar Linton had difficulty in hearing me to the close. His first
words revealed that he did not clear his wife of blame.
