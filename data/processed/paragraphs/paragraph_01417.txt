“I can get over the wall,” she said laughing. “The Grange is
not a prison, Ellen, and you are not my gaoler. And besides, I’m almost
seventeen: I’m a woman. And I’m certain Linton would recover
quickly if he had me to look after him. I’m older than he is, you know,
and wiser: less childish, am I not? And he’ll soon do as I direct him,
with some slight coaxing. He’s a pretty little darling when he’s
good. I’d make such a pet of him, if he were mine. We should never
quarrel, should we, after we were used to each other? Don’t you like him,
Ellen?”
