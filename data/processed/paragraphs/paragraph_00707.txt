“Among his books!” she cried, confounded. “And I dying! I on
the brink of the grave! My God! does he know how I’m altered?”
continued she, staring at her reflection in a mirror hanging against the
opposite wall. “Is that Catherine Linton? He imagines me in a
pet—in play, perhaps. Cannot you inform him that it is frightful earnest?
Nelly, if it be not too late, as soon as I learn how he feels, I’ll
choose between these two: either to starve at once—that would be no
punishment unless he had a heart—or to recover, and leave the country.
Are you speaking the truth about him now? Take care. Is he actually so utterly
indifferent for my life?”
