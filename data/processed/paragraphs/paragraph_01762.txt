I left him there, and proceeded down the valley alone. The grey church looked
greyer, and the lonely churchyard lonelier. I distinguished a moor-sheep
cropping the short turf on the graves. It was sweet, warm weather—too
warm for travelling; but the heat did not hinder me from enjoying the
delightful scenery above and below: had I seen it nearer August, I’m sure
it would have tempted me to waste a month among its solitudes. In winter
nothing more dreary, in summer nothing more divine, than those glens shut in by
hills, and those bluff, bold swells of heath.
