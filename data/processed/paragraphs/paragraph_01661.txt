“I know he has a bad nature,” said Catherine: “he’s
your son. But I’m glad I’ve a better, to forgive it; and I know he
loves me, and for that reason I love him. Mr. Heathcliff, you have nobody to love you; and, however miserable you make us, we shall still
have the revenge of thinking that your cruelty arises from your greater misery.
You are miserable, are you not? Lonely, like the devil, and envious like
him? Nobody loves you— nobody will cry for you when you die!
I wouldn’t be you!”
