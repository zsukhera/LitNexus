“Look!” she cried eagerly, “that’s my room with the
candle in it, and the trees swaying before it; and the other candle is in
Joseph’s garret. Joseph sits up late, doesn’t he? He’s
waiting till I come home that he may lock the gate. Well, he’ll wait a
while yet. It’s a rough journey, and a sad heart to travel it; and we
must pass by Gimmerton Kirk to go that journey! We’ve braved its ghosts
often together, and dared each other to stand among the graves and ask them to
come. But, Heathcliff, if I dare you now, will you venture? If you do,
I’ll keep you. I’ll not lie there by myself: they may bury me
twelve feet deep, and throw the church down over me, but I won’t rest
till you are with me. I never will!”
