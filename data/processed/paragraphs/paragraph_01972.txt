I saw nothing; but neither the sheep nor he would go on, so I bid him take the
road lower down. He probably raised the phantoms from thinking, as he traversed
the moors alone, on the nonsense he had heard his parents and companions
repeat. Yet, still, I don’t like being out in the dark now; and I
don’t like being left by myself in this grim house: I cannot help it; I
shall be glad when they leave it, and shift to the Grange.
