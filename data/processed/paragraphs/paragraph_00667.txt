“Oh, the evil is that I am not jealous, is it?” cried
Catherine. “Well, I won’t repeat my offer of a wife: it is as bad
as offering Satan a lost soul. Your bliss lies, like his, in inflicting misery.
You prove it. Edgar is restored from the ill-temper he gave way to at your
coming; I begin to be secure and tranquil; and you, restless to know us at
peace, appear resolved on exciting a quarrel. Quarrel with Edgar, if you
please, Heathcliff, and deceive his sister: you’ll hit on exactly the
most efficient method of revenging yourself on me.”
