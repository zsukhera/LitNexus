“It is strange,” I began, in the interval of swallowing one cup of
tea and receiving another—“it is strange how custom can mould our
tastes and ideas: many could not imagine the existence of happiness in a life
of such complete exile from the world as you spend, Mr. Heathcliff; yet,
I’ll venture to say, that, surrounded by your family, and with your
amiable lady as the presiding genius over your home and heart—”
