While enjoying a month of fine weather at the sea-coast, I was thrown into the
company of a most fascinating creature: a real goddess in my eyes, as long as
she took no notice of me. I “never told my love” vocally; still, if
looks have language, the merest idiot might have guessed I was over head and
ears: she understood me at last, and looked a return—the sweetest of all
imaginable looks. And what did I do? I confess it with shame—shrunk icily
into myself, like a snail; at every glance retired colder and farther; till
finally the poor innocent was led to doubt her own senses, and, overwhelmed
with confusion at her supposed mistake, persuaded her mamma to decamp.
