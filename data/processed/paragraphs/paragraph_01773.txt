I would have asked why Mrs. Dean had deserted the Grange, but it was impossible
to delay her at such a crisis, so I turned away and made my exit, rambling
leisurely along, with the glow of a sinking sun behind, and the mild glory of a
rising moon in front—one fading, and the other brightening—as I
quitted the park, and climbed the stony by-road branching off to Mr.
Heathcliff’s dwelling. Before I arrived in sight of it, all that remained
of day was a beamless amber light along the west: but I could see every pebble
on the path, and every blade of grass, by that splendid moon. I had neither to
climb the gate nor to knock—it yielded to my hand. That is an
improvement, I thought. And I noticed another, by the aid of my nostrils; a
fragrance of stocks and wallflowers wafted on the air from amongst the homely
fruit-trees.
