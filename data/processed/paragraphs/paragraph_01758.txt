1802.—This September I was invited to devastate the moors of a friend in
the north, and on my journey to his abode, I unexpectedly came within fifteen
miles of Gimmerton. The ostler at a roadside public-house was holding a pail of
water to refresh my horses, when a cart of very green oats, newly reaped,
passed by, and he remarked,—“Yon’s frough Gimmerton, nah!
They’re allas three wick’ after other folk wi’ ther
harvest.”
