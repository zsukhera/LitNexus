“I think he swore: but I didn’t mind him, I was straining to see
the bairn,” and she began again to describe it rapturously. I, as zealous
as herself, hurried eagerly home to admire, on my part; though I was very sad
for Hindley’s sake. He had room in his heart only for two idols—his
wife and himself: he doted on both, and adored one, and I couldn’t
conceive how he would bear the loss.
