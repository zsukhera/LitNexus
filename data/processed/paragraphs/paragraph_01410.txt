“I told you I did,” he replied impatiently. “Sit on the
settle and let me lean on your knee. That’s as mamma used to do, whole
afternoons together. Sit quite still and don’t talk: but you may sing a
song, if you can sing; or you may say a nice long interesting ballad—one
of those you promised to teach me; or a story. I’d rather have a ballad,
though: begin.”
