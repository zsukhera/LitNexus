On Easter Monday, Joseph went to Gimmerton fair with some cattle; and, in the
afternoon, I was busy getting up linen in the kitchen. Earnshaw sat, morose as
usual, at the chimney corner, and my little mistress was beguiling an idle hour
with drawing pictures on the window-panes, varying her amusement by smothered
bursts of songs, and whispered ejaculations, and quick glances of annoyance and
impatience in the direction of her cousin, who steadfastly smoked, and looked
into the grate. At a notice that I could do with her no longer intercepting my
light, she removed to the hearthstone. I bestowed little attention on her
proceedings, but, presently, I heard her begin—“I’ve found
out, Hareton, that I want—that I’m glad—that I should like
you to be my cousin now, if you had not grown so cross to me, and so
rough.”
