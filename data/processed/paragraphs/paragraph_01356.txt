“Is that you, Miss Linton?” he said, raising his head from the arm
of the great chair, in which he reclined. “No—don’t kiss me:
it takes my breath. Dear me! Papa said you would call,” continued he,
after recovering a little from Catherine’s embrace; while she stood by
looking very contrite. “Will you shut the door, if you please? you left
it open; and those—those detestable creatures won’t bring
coals to the fire. It’s so cold!”
