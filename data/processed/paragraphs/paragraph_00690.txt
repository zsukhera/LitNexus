The stolidity with which I received these instructions was, no doubt, rather
exasperating: for they were delivered in perfect sincerity; but I believed a
person who could plan the turning of her fits of passion to account,
beforehand, might, by exerting her will, manage to control herself tolerably,
even while under their influence; and I did not wish to “frighten”
her husband, as she said, and multiply his annoyances for the purpose of
serving her selfishness. Therefore I said nothing when I met the master coming
towards the parlour; but I took the liberty of turning back to listen whether
they would resume their quarrel together. He began to speak first.
