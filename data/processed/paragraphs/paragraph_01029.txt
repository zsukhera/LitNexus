Mr. Heathcliff, meeting me one day in the village, inquired where she lived. I
refused to tell. He remarked that it was not of any moment, only she must
beware of coming to her brother: she should not be with him, if he had to keep
her himself. Though I would give no information, he discovered, through some of
the other servants, both her place of residence and the existence of the child.
Still, he didn’t molest her: for which forbearance she might thank his
aversion, I suppose. He often asked about the infant, when he saw me; and on
hearing its name, smiled grimly, and observed: “They wish me to hate it
too, do they?”
