“I see in you, Nelly,” she continued dreamily, “an aged
woman: you have grey hair and bent shoulders. This bed is the fairy cave under
Penistone Crags, and you are gathering elf-bolts to hurt our heifers;
pretending, while I am near, that they are only locks of wool. That’s
what you’ll come to fifty years hence: I know you are not so now.
I’m not wandering: you’re mistaken, or else I should believe you
really were that withered hag, and I should think I was under
Penistone Crags; and I’m conscious it’s night, and there are two
candles on the table making the black press shine like jet.”
