The young man evidently thought it too bad that he should be laughed at for his
ignorance, and then laughed at for trying to remove it. I had a similar notion;
and, remembering Mrs. Dean’s anecdote of his first attempt at
enlightening the darkness in which he had been reared, I
observed,—“But, Mrs. Heathcliff, we have each had a commencement,
and each stumbled and tottered on the threshold; had our teachers scorned
instead of aiding us, we should stumble and totter yet.”
